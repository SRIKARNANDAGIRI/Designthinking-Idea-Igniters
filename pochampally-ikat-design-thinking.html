Reframe Check

[Introductory note — e.g. "Before finalising our problem statement, we challenged our assumptions and looked at alternative framings of the issue."]

Conventional Frame

[CONVENTIONAL FRAME 1 — e.g. "Power looms are the villain; handlooms are the victim."]

[Why this frame is limiting — e.g. "This adversarial framing ignores that power loom operators are also small entrepreneurs responding to market demand. Blaming one group rarely yields systemic solutions."]

Reframed Perspective

[REFRAME 1 — e.g. "The real problem is a broken information market — consumers can't price authenticity."]

[Why this reframe is richer — e.g. "If buyers could easily identify and trust authentic Ikat, they might willingly pay a premium — making coexistence with power looms possible without requiring regulation or conflict."]

Conventional Frame

[CONVENTIONAL FRAME 2 — e.g. "Artisans need government subsidies and protection."]

[Why this is limiting — e.g. "Subsidy dependence rarely creates sustainable change. Artisans themselves have identified dignity, fair prices, and direct market access as their priority — not charity."]

Reframed Perspective

[REFRAME 2 — e.g. "Artisans need market infrastructure — tools to sell their story, not just their product."]

[E.g. "The craft's narrative — the [X] days, the resist-dyeing process, the family lineage — is the product's most valuable differentiator. Enabling artisans to communicate this story directly to buyers is the leverage point."]

✅ Selected Concept Moving to Prototype

Selected Concept

[SELECTED CONCEPT NAME — e.g. "Ikat Identity: A QR-Linked Artisan Authentication System"]

[Describe the concept in 3–4 sentences. E.g. "A lightweight system where each handloom saree is tagged with a scannable QR code — woven or attached at point of completion — that links to a digital artisan passport showing the weaver's name, village, weaving process timeline, and GI certification status. The consumer scans at purchase and sees exactly who made it and how. A simple dashboard lets artisans update their profile via WhatsApp, eliminating the need for smartphones or digital literacy."]

Why This Concept?

[Rationale for selection — e.g. "This concept directly addresses the root cause identified in the 5-Whys analysis: the information asymmetry between artisans and consumers. It is low-cost, scalable, compatible with existing sales channels, and does not require artisans to change their production process."]
